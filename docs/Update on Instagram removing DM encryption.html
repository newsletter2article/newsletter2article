Hello, Reader

Last month Meta quietly shared that they plan to stop supporting end-to-end encryption on Instagram messages.

Mozilla Foundation broke the news with a video on Instagram to let people know what they can do to protect their privacy because of this plan. More than 1 million people watched our video, 200,000 people shared it, and 22,244 people signed our petition asking Meta to reverse this decision.

That got Meta's attention. We met with Meta to discuss our petition and get answers to your questions. Here's what we learned:

Instagram encryption will definitely disappear on May 8, 2026

Meta confirmed to us that end-to-end encryption will be removed from Instagram DMs, and that the decision is final. The last day for Instagram DM encryption is May 8.

Their official position: not enough people used encrypted Instagram DMs, and people who want encryption can go to WhatsApp instead.

Mozilla Foundation's take: This is a bummer. Encryption should be the default. And it's no surprise that Meta doesn't think enough people used encryption on Instagram—they made it hard to find.

Encrypted Instagram DMs will be deleted, not decrypted

Many people wondered what would happen to their encrypted chats. Some good news here: previously encrypted chats will not be decrypted (and can’t be).

However, after May 8, 2026, those messages will effectively disappear. You have until then to save those messages for yourself by following Meta's instructions.

WhatsApp and Messenger remain encrypted*

Meta confirmed the Instagram decision has no impact on WhatsApp or Messenger encryption—those remain intact, and they encourage people who prioritize encryption to use WhatsApp.

*An important note: Messenger is only encrypted for conversations between two individuals. Group chats are not yet encrypted, and neither are chats with business accounts. So even a group of you and two friends on Messenger is not actually encrypted. Our advice is to be mindful of this when you’re choosing messaging providers—we suggest you move family and group chats somewhere more secure.

The company says they're working on bringing encryption to more types of messages on Messenger, but did not offer specifics on timing. We'll keep watching this issue.

Meta says private message content is not used for ad targeting or AI training

Many people expressed concerns that this move was about giving AI more data to train on (i.e., your Instagram DMs) or that Meta would use messages for monetization purposes. Meta gave two clear answers here:

  • Ads: Meta says they do not not use the content of personal DMs (1:1 or group chats with personal accounts) for ad personalization.
  • AI training: Meta also says they don't use private message content to train AI—unless someone in the chat actively shares those messages with a Meta AI. This applies across WhatsApp, Messenger, and Instagram.

When it comes to AI training, the line isn't necessarily private vs. public—it's what you choose to share with AI features. The moment you interact with Meta AI in a chat, that conversation becomes fodder.

Use Signal for Encrypted Communication

Instagram DMs were never the gold standard for privacy. WhatsApp remains more private than Instagram, but it's still a Meta product—that means the same data infrastructure, the same AI tool integrations in chats, and the same corporate incentives.

Mozilla Foundation's strong recommendation is that Signal is the best way to securely communicate with end-to-end encryption as the default. You could also use a service like Threema. (And after recent findings about notification storage, remember to actively turn off your message preview notifications for all your messaging services.)

Encryption Needs Active, Vocal Support

The future of technology needs to be powered by people. While it's good news that Meta says encryption in WhatsApp and Messenger is safe for now, it's clear that we can't rely on profit-prioritizing companies to protect core values of the Internet like privacy.

That's why Mozilla Foundation is building a better technology future—powered by people, open by design, and fueled by imagination. Technology needs to serve people, and people who use technology need to help shape it.
