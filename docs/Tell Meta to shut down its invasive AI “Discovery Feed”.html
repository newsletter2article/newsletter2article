Hello, Reader

People are turning to AI chatbots for much more than polishing their work emails  — like job advice, relationship questions, and even to share personal photos.1 Many people assume those queries stay between them and their screens. But what if those AI conversations were suddenly broadcast to the world?

Meta recently launched an AI assistant with a social “Discover Feed” — a public stream of conversations users had with the AI.2

The result?

Incredibly sensitive, deeply personal messages — medical concerns, relationship confessions, legal anxieties, even exact locations — are ending up online, tied to real people’s profiles. Meta says users consented to share, but some users say they had no idea their AI conversations could be made public.3, 4

That’s why the Mozilla community is calling on Meta to shut down the Discover feed until real privacy protections are in place. If enough of us add our voices, we can generate enough public pressure to push Meta to take action.

Add your name to demand Meta suspend its invasive AI feed until the company can guarantee no private conversations are made public without clear, explicit, and informed opt-in consent.

Sign now →
 

Together we’re calling on Meta to:

  • Shut down the Discover Feed until real privacy protections are in place.
  • Make all AI interactions private by default with no public sharing option unless explicitly enabled through informed consent.
  • Provide full transparency about how many users have unknowingly shared private information.
  • Create a universal, easy-to-use opt-out system for all Meta platforms that prevents user data from being used for AI training.
  • Notify all users whose conversations may have been made public, and allow them to delete their content permanently.
 

Privacy, especially sensitive information we give to a chatbot, shouldn’t be a setting buried in a menu — it's a basic right.

From the deeply personal posts visible in Meta's AI "Discovery Feed," it's not clear that all users know their queries are visible for the world to see. Meta needs to make it easier for people to control their privacy.

At Mozilla Foundation, we believe in a future where AI is worthy of our trust — where your data is handled with care, and your choices are honored by design. That means building tech that's transparent about when and how your data is shared. People deserve to know when their conversations are public, especially when they believe they're speaking in private.

This is a moment to raise the bar. Let's demand better defaults and an internet that respects your voice, your choices, and your boundaries — starting with Meta’s invasive new AI Discover Feed.

P.S. While we work together to hold Meta accountable, here are some quick privacy tips for how to protect your privacy when using AI chatbots.

  • Try using the accountless version of AI chatbots when possible
  • Avoid "Sign in with Google/Facebook" options (Apple's sign-in is generally safer)
  • Turn off automatic data-sharing in your phone or browser settings
  • Opt out of AI training whenever that option is available
  • Don't share anything with an AI that you want to keep private
  • And finally: Sign the petition to tell Meta to stop turning private chats into public content without meaningful opt-in informed consent.
