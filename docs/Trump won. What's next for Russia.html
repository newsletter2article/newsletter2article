Donald Trump’s victory in the U.S. presidential election has created even more uncertainty about the future of the war in Ukraine. Russian officials welcomed the news with caution, hoping that it might shift the balance of the war in their favor but remembering Washington's tough stance on Russia during his first term.

Trump has long bragged that he could bring Russian and Ukrainian leadership together and end the war within 24 hours of becoming president.

The Wall Street Journal reported that the president-elect’s advisers have proposed solutions that would allow Russia to continue occupying 20% of Ukrainian territory and bar Ukraine from NATO membership for 20 years, along with the establishment of a 1,300-kilometer-long demilitarized zone.

Here’s what else happened this week:

  • Russia’s Central Bank hinted that its key interest rate may rise again next month. It is already at a record high of 21%.
  • A Russian general in charge of a unit that allegedly tortured its own soldiers and murdered a U.S. citizen was reportedly killed in Ukraine.
  • Police carried out new raids on the Romani community in the Chelyabinsk region, seizing weapons and handing out military summons.
  • Russia captured another village as it advances toward the key logistics hub of Pokrovsk in the Donetsk region.
  • An official said that 3,300 foreigners have been awarded Russian citizenship after fighting in Ukraine.
